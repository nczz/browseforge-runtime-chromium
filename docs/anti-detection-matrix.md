Status vocabulary: `pass` means committed evidence exists; `warn`/`warning` means accepted alpha risk with evidence; `fail` means evidence shows a defect; `not_tested` means no detector evidence exists; `not_applicable` means the surface does not apply; `blocked` means required artifact/evidence is missing or failed operationally; `manual` means the artifact exists but validation requires the target OS or operator step.

macOS x64 has a real `browseforge-runtime-chromium-v0.1.0-alpha.0-macos-x64.zip` with SHA-256, SBOM, and provenance. It remains `not_tested` for launch and detector evidence by maintainer decision, and it must be described as packaged-only / unsigned / not launch-tested / not detector-tested. Do not mark detector surfaces `pass` without separate detector evidence.

Linux arm64 has a real `browseforge-runtime-chromium-v0.1.0-alpha.0-linux-arm64.zip` with SHA-256, SBOM, provenance, BrowseForge status/profile launch, REST, MCP, and direct headed Docker detector evidence. It remains `packaged_pending_smoke_detector` / non-production because iphey is blocked by collector failure, proxy/native-host detector rows remain gaps, and Playwright Bind has not been independently exercised.
